Top 3 Must-Do Experiences

Live Palma like a local

Lose Yourself in Palma’s Old Town

Wander through narrow cobblestone streets lined with ancient Gothic palaces, hidden courtyards (patios), and artisan shops.

Swim in a Secret Cala

Skip the crowded beaches and head to Cala Varques (east coast)—turquoise water, white cliffs, and hidden caves to explore.

Sunset at Cap de Formentor

Drive the twisting road to the northernmost point of the island for panoramic views of the Tramuntana Mountains meeting the sea.

Gastronomy with Soul

Tastes that tell stories

Pa amb oli

The island’s iconic bread with olive oil, crushed tomato, and local toppings (sobrassada, cheese, or anchovies).

Tumbet

A veggie lover’s dream—layers of eggplant, potatoes, and peppers in a rich tomato sauce.

Frito Mallorquín

A hearty fry-up with lamb, liver, and potatoes (adventurous eaters only!).

Ensaimada

The sweet, spiral pastry you’ll crave for breakfast (or dessert!).
